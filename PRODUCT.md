# venaritravel: Product Context

Synthesized from `Design_Spec_venaritravel.md` and the master prompt that briefed this build. Source-of-truth for tone and direction; pair with `DESIGN.md` for visual tokens.

## What this is

A single-URL landing page (`/`) for **venaritravel**, a luxury Spanish hunting travel agency. The site does not transact: it is a touchpoint that filters in the right kind of client and opens a private conversation. Page composition is editorial, slow, and reserved, closer to a typographic book than a tourism site.

The brand is anchored in *places* (dehesas, sierras, casonas, fincas) rather than promises ("luxury", "exclusivity", "experiences"). Heritage and territory do the work that copy does in lesser brands.

## Register

**Brand.** The design IS the product. Visitors form an impression of refinement, discretion, and material quality before they ever read a sentence. The page should feel like it was art-directed, not assembled.

## Audience

European and Latin-American clients who already know hunting (often the third or fourth generation in their family to do it) and value not having to organize anything. The page does not sell hunting. It establishes credibility and atmosphere; the conversation closes the relationship.

The visitor should leave with one feeling: *they understand what I want without me having to explain it.*

## Tone of voice

- **Sustracción antes que ornamento.** Sentences are short, declarative, and earn their place. Subordinate clauses are a luxury.
- **Materia antes que mensaje.** Copy names concrete materials and places (cordobán, cuero, dehesa, Sierra de Gredos), not abstract feelings.
- **Lugar antes que promesa.** No "experiencias únicas", "momentos inolvidables", or any tourism vocabulary. No defending hunting or critiquing the sector.
- The brand never mentions ethics, never apologizes, never uses ALL CAPS for emphasis, never uses exclamation marks.

## Strategic principles

1. **Discretion is the product.** Anything that draws attention to itself (loud color, motion, microcopy) erodes the proposition. Restraint with intent.
2. **Material > promise.** Imagery of leather, tweed, oiled wood, paper, and Spanish countryside light carries the weight that adjectives carry in lesser brands.
3. **Anchored in place.** Specific Spanish geography (dehesas extremeñas, sierras béticas, casonas castellanas) is the differentiator. No generic "Europe" or "Spain" framing.
4. **Lineal, no creativo.** The page reads top-to-bottom, no carousels, no tabs, no clever interactions. The user follows the editor, not the interface.

## Anti-references (visual + copy)

Reject any of these reflexes immediately:

- Outdoor-Americana saturation (Filson, Beretta-influencer aesthetic): bright palettes, tactical wear, blaze orange, camo.
- Drone-real-estate atmosphere ("aerial swooping over property at golden hour").
- Influencer-lifestyle staging: group selfies, guests laughing at camera, posed portraits.
- Stock-trip-imagery clichés: smiling models in tweed, propped shotgun-with-gloves shots, Photoshopped golden-hour-everything.
- Editorial drop caps + italic display + ruled-separator template (Klim-style "designy" fingerprint). The brand is editorial in *register*, not in *aesthetic costume*.
- Trophies, blood, kill imagery as primary subjects.
- Logos visible on apparel or equipment (Beretta, Barbour, Filson). Avoid all branded gear.
- Words like *bespoke*, *curated experience*, *journey*, *passion*, *heritage*. The brand shows; it does not declare.

## What success looks like

A visitor who scrolls the page once and decides: "I want to talk to these people." The contact form gets fewer submissions than a normal landing page, and each one is qualified.

## Reference clients (mood, not copy)

- **Holland & Holland** editorial campaigns of the last decade: light, weather, restraint.
- **Aman Resorts** photography: client never the protagonist; the place is.
- **How To Spend It (FT)**: composition, white space, page rhythm.
- **The Field / Gentleman's Journal (UK)**: country sports tradition done right.
- **Loewe Craft Prize films**: attention to material, hands working, time.

These are *references for self-evaluation*, not templates to imitate. If the rendered page could be confused for any of them, that is a sign of taste, not plagiarism. If it could be confused for a SaaS or a Stripe-adjacent landing, the work has failed.
